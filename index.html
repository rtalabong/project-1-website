Overanalyzing my green card petition

Millions of immigration documents are processed each year. Mine is one of them.
This is an attempt at understanding the dizzying process and its infamous wait times.

By Rambo Talabong

The immigration process is stressful. This is a rant I have had ever since I moved to the United States in the fall of 2023.

Some people can wait for months, while many end up waiting for years, decades even.

I recently joined immigration purgatory after I was petitioned by my husband through the form I-130, the aim being that I get a green card so I can stay in the US permanently.

We submitted our petition in February 2024. We have been waiting for 5 months â€“ not nearly as long as many others who are waiting, but still long enough to make me wonder.

As I am in this wait, I have been learning how to improve my storytelling with the help of data through Columbia's Lede Program.

For my first project, I thought: What better way to put these skills to use than to expound on my complaint? And so I have this project, using data from the US Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).

First, I aimed to show the scale and the direction of the process. How many are we in this? How does it start and how does it end? For this, I made the chart below.

Next: Just like most of the people with an immigration petition, I am most preoccupied by the speed. How soon can I get my green card?

With this in mind, I wanted to know how the immigration processing speed changed from one administration to another, starting with Obama's last term.

With Biden possibly being replaced by Trump in the next presidential election, I wanted to know if it would affect my wait time. And if so, how much?

The only way to do this was by comparing the immigration speeds of each administration. Fortunately, they were recorded every year.

It appears to be that Trump slowed down the processing of most immigration documents following Obama. Biden ended up being slower, but he also inherited a backlog from Trump during the pandemic.

Because I am prone to overthinking, I also wanted to find out one more thing: Does filing at a lucky time matter? Are there months when the USCIS processes documents faster?

If so, I wanted to know whether within Biden's time as president, there were notable fluctuations in processing time based on when one made their submission.

Luckily, the USCIS published the quarterly processing times of immigration documents.

For immigration documents in our category, processing time did not fluctuate significantly from quarter to quarter. However, it is a different story for different immigration forms.

This personal project by no means answers all immigration questions. It does not even answer all of mine.

If I had the data, I would have loved to find out the difference in denial rates among the three presidents I looked at. (Just because Trump was faster did not mean he approved more than he denied.)

Immigration is also a multifaceted phenomenon. Immigration processing time is not the only metric to judge a president's immigration policy.

For me at least, I know that fluctuations from president to president or quarter to quarter within Biden's term are not significant enough that I should be worried about waiting for years.
